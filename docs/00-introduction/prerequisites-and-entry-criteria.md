## Prerequisites

:::warning[Before you start]
Mastery of these prerequisites is critical for effective and safe GenAI & LLM Documentation adoption.
:::

- **Core Skills**: Proficiency in Logic, Language, Systems, and Sentences (as defined in `docs/01-core-skills/`).
- **GenAI Literacy**: Understanding of LLM capabilities, limitations, and failure modes (e.g., hallucinations, context window limits).
- **Domain Knowledge**: Sufficient understanding of the problem domain and technical stack.

## Self-Check: Can You Apply GenAI & LLM Documentation Safely?

Ask yourself these questions before starting any GenAI & LLM Documentation task:

-   Can I articulate the exact intent and desired outcome of this task?
-   Can I list all non-negotiable constraints (technical, security, business)?
-   Do I understand the system context where this change will live?
-   Can I define clear, objective acceptance criteria for the output?
-   Am I prepared to critically review the AI's output and take full accountability?

If you answer "No" to any of these, pause and strengthen that area.

---

## Entry Criteria

To begin applying GenAI & LLM Documentation effectively:

-   You **MUST** be able to articulate the Intent Spec for your task.
-   You **MUST** be able to define the Constraint Spec for your task.
-   You **MUST** understand the need for evidence-based verification.
-   You **MUST** accept full accountability for the outcome of AI-assisted work.

If you are just starting your journey, focus on building these foundational skills. Re-enter GenAI & LLM Documentation after you can produce: intent, constraints, review plan, and evidence.

---

## Beyond the Basics

Prerequisites include **both cognitive/process skills and minimum GenAI literacy**. GenAI & LLM Documentation assumes you understand probabilistic behavior, context limits, and the necessity of verification before shipping.
